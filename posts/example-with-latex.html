Understanding Mathematical Equations

This is an example blog post demonstrating LaTeX support. You can write mathematical equations using standard LaTeX notation.

Inline Equations

You can write inline equations like Einstein's famous equation $E = mc^2$, or the quadratic formula $x = \frac{-b \pm \sqrt{b^2 - 4ac}}{2a}$.

Display Equations

For larger equations, use display mode:

$$ \int_{-\infty}^{\infty} e^{-x^2} dx = \sqrt{\pi} $$

Or the definition of the derivative:

$$ f'(x) = \lim_{h \to 0} \frac{f(x+h) - f(x)}{h} $$

Complex Equations

You can write more complex mathematical expressions:

$$ \sum_{i=1}^{n} i = \frac{n(n+1)}{2} $$

The Euler-Lagrange equation:

$$ \frac{\partial L}{\partial q} - \frac{d}{dt}\frac{\partial L}{\partial \dot{q}} = 0 $$

Matrices and Vectors

You can also write matrices:

$$ \begin{bmatrix} a & b \\ c & d \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} x \\ y \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} ax + by \\ cx + dy \end{bmatrix} $$

This demonstrates how easy it is to include mathematical notation in your blog posts!
